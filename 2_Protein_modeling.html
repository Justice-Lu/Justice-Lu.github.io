Exploring Protein Modeling

Goal

The goal of this vignette is to provide some basic understanding of protein modleing using python.
Here I am interested in comparing how does the protein model generated by Alphafold compare to the Cryo-EM structure.

Protein data files

When handling protein data. Depending on the preprocess, it's generally stored in the PDB file format. PDB files usually consists of the main information including the atom and the corresponding coordinates in a three dimensional space. And together all of these cooredinates is what makes up of a protein model in the 3D space.

ATOM      2  H   MET A   1      32.761  -7.721 -34.975  1.00 34.30           H

ATOM      6  HA  MET A   1      35.239  -7.287 -33.439  1.00 34.30           H

There are a variety of method in obtaining protein structure (PDB file). Some include an actual true structure from cystalzation, and some are predictions via predictor models such as Alphafold.

Visualizing Protein structure

A simple way in visualizing the protein structure is simply plotting all the 3D coordinates. However, more often than not, doing so will result in a uninterpretable giant cloud due to the number of atoms there are. Instead it's often more intuitive to simply plot the backbone of the amin acid not including the side chains.

Below is simply an interactive plot overlaying the two protein structures generated by either Cryo-EM or alphafold. Feel free to rotate around and explore the structure!

From the model it looks like the models generated from the two sources are quite similar which is a good start. However, olfactory receptor and protein folding in general is rather sensitive and selective. Any small movement or mutations deviating from the original form may lead to loss of function or failture to bind to ligands.
