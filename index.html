About me

I am currently a PhD candidate at Duke University in the department of Molecular Genetics and Microbiology advised by Dr. Hiroaki Matsunami.

My interest lies in understanding how our perception of smell may be influenced by the micro-organisms residing in our nose. More broadly, I am motivated to learn and utilize computational framework to analyze and transform complex datasets into a visually intuitive manner.

My expertise includes python, R, single cell sequencing and protein modeling. I'm driven to become a well rounded data scientist to bridge the gap between biological research and data.

Goal

My main motivation is to not only make biological research data more intuitive and understandable by the general public but also finding different strategies to analyze and visualize datasets.

For example imagine a mice presented with a flower, and ask the question of how do we know if the mice is smelling the flower?
In order to quantitatively measure how much the mice perceive the floural smell I incoporate experimental generated biological readouts with computational data wrangling to translate complex biological data into a variety of intepreble diagrams.

In this website I will update some of the small projects that I have worked with on the side to hopefully showcase some basic tutorials.
